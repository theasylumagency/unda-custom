04 — WHAT AN OPERATOR CAN TAKE ON

An operator does not need to run your whole business.

It needs to take responsibility for the right part of it.

Start with one clear area of work. Define what should keep moving, what the operator can handle and when a person should step in.

SOCIAL MEDIA OPERATOR

Responsibility

Keep the brand’s social presence active, consistent and aligned with the business.

Watches

The content calendar, brand context, upcoming offers, events and approved priorities.

Does

Plans the weekly content, prepares posts, coordinates review and publishes approved material on schedule.

Brings to you

New campaigns, sensitive brand decisions and anything that needs direct founder input.

CUSTOMER REQUEST OPERATOR

Responsibility

Keep incoming customer requests moving from the first message to the right next action.

Watches

Messages, website enquiries, customer information, product or service details and previous conversations.

Does

Understands the request, checks the relevant information, prepares or sends permitted responses, updates the record and follows up when needed.

Brings to you

Unusual requests, complaints, special conditions and decisions outside its defined limits.

Different businesses need different operators.

The principle stays the same: one clear responsibility, the right context and defined limits.
